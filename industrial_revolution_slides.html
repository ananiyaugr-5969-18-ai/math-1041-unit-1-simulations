The Industrial Domino Effect

The Industrial Revolution was not a single event, but a sequential chain reaction of technological and organizational changes.

One breakthrough inevitably exposed a bottleneck in another area, driving further innovation.

1. The Textile Domino

  • Spinning: Spinning jenny/mule created a surplus of yarn.
  • Weaving: Surplus yarn required the power-loom to speed up weaving.
  • Finishing: Increased cloth production necessitated chemical breakthroughs in bleaching.

2. The Power Cycle

Efficiency demands led to the Steam Engine.

Steam required coal → Coal mining required deeper excavations → Deep mines required steam pumps.

This closed loop fueled the growth of heavy industry.

3. Transportation Expansion

Steam moved from factories to the tracks and oceans.

  • Locomotives and Steamships maximized trade capacity.
  • Global markets became accessible for the first time.

4. Precision & Social Shift

Machining: Interchangable parts and precision tools allowed mass production.

The Factory System: Pulled workers into cities, creating the modern industrial social structure.
