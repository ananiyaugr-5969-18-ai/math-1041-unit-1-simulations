Mitosis: The Stages of Cell Division

A visual guide to the identical replication of somatic cells.

Prophase

  • Condensation: Chromosomes become visible and condensed.
  • Centrioles: Separate and move to opposite poles.
  • Envelope: The nuclear membrane dissolves.
  • Attachment: Spindle fibers attach to sister chromatids.

Metaphase

  • Alignment: Chromosomes line up at the centeral "equator".
  • Connection: Spindle fibers connect fully to centromeres.

Anaphase

  • Separation: Sister chromatids separate into individual chromosomes.
  • Movement: Shortening fibers pull chromatids to opposite poles.
  • Result: Ensures identical sets in both future daughter cells.

Telophase

  • Reformation: New nuclear envelopes form around each set.
  • Decondensation: Chromosomes return to chromatin state.
  • Reconstruction: The mitotic spindle breaks down.

Followed by Cytokinesis: Final cytoplasmic division.
